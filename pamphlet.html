Learn About Diabetic Amputation

Step 1: Understand the Risks

Recognize how diabetes can lead to complications like nerve damage or poor circulation.

Step 2: Monitor Blood Sugar

Track your glucose levels regularly to prevent spikes that damage nerves.

Step 3: Inspect Feet Daily

Look for cuts, sores, and swelling to catch infections early.

Step 4: Wear Proper Footwear

Use diabetic-friendly shoes to avoid pressure points and injuries.

Step 5: Exercise Regularly

Movement helps with circulation and controlling blood sugar levels.

Step 6: Manage Diet

Choose foods that stabilize glucose and promote healing.

Step 7: Routine Doctor Visits

Early intervention can prevent escalation of foot-related issues.

Step 8: Know Warning Signs

Recognize numbness, discoloration, or chronic pain as red flags.
