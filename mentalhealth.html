Mental Health & Wellbeing

Click a topic to learn more about managing your mental health while living with diabetes.

Anxiety

Practice grounding techniques like deep breathing or the 5-4-3-2-1 method. Stay active and seek peer support.

Visit ADAA for Help

Depression

Start small with daily goals. Journaling and talking to a professional can help lift the fog.

Visit NAMI

Diabetes Burnout

It’s okay to take breaks. Reconnect with your ‘why’ and talk to others managing diabetes too.

Diabetes.org Guide

Chronic Stress

Stretch, sleep, and set boundaries. Daily stress logs can help track triggers and progress.

Mental Health Fact Cards

People with diabetes are 2–3x more likely to develop depression.
Exercise releases endorphins, which boost your mood naturally.
Gratitude journaling can help lower stress levels significantly.
Sleep plays a key role in emotional regulation and insulin sensitivity.
Talking to someone you trust can lighten emotional burdens.
Even 5 minutes of deep breathing can reduce cortisol levels.
Loneliness can impact your physical health—stay socially connected.
Practicing mindfulness helps manage both stress and blood sugar.
Sticking to a routine can help reduce anxiety and improve focus.
Drinking water and moving often reduces fatigue and stress.
Visualizing positive outcomes can improve mental resilience.
Nature exposure—even photos—can reduce heart rate and stress.
You’re not alone—millions manage diabetes and thrive every day.
Music therapy has been shown to relieve diabetic distress.
Laughter boosts immunity and lowers blood pressure. Watch a comedy!
Mental health check-ins are just as important as glucose checks.
